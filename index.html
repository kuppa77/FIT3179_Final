Tourism in Australia in 2025

Context, Trends and Patterns

Visitor Trends

International tourism in Australia was very strong pre-Covid in 2018-2019, December 2018 was the strongest showing in years, with 1.06 million tourists arriving, and a strong showing in that summer.

Tourism plumetted in Covid, with December 2021 brining in a Million less people than that of 2018, with its peak in June 2022 of 0.28 million.

Totals began to rise only in late 2022, with numbers gradually increasing throughout 2023, until finally reaching similar levels in mid 2024.

The year of 2024-25 is the first year that numbers are almost identical to pre-covid figures, with 0.95 million visitors in December 2024

The strongest month annualy is December, with tourism picking up from November through to February, before it dips to its lowest in early winter, sepcifically in May and June.

Where are they coming from?

Visitor Demographics

New Zealanders are the most frequent visitors of Australia, with 1.2 million vsitors annualy. This is likely due to its proximity, which also leads to these visitors not spending many nights in Australia.

Results are similar for South East Asian countries, also frequent visitors who tend to spend fewer nights.

Visitors from China and India are also frequent, but they usually have much longer visits.

Baring the UK Australia is not a frequent visit for Europeans, likely due to the distance. Visitors from France and Italy ensure they stay for longer, to make the far the distance worth it.

Americans and Candians also frequent Australia, but usally do not stay for extended periods.

Country Trends over Time

Country Trends

The biggest shift has come from India which increased its visitors by 200,000 over 10 years. It is now the 5th most popular origin country.

The USA, New Zealand and South Korea all grew moderately, each by roughly 150,000 visitors. New Zealand comfortably remains the most frequent visitors of Australia.

The next group contains Indonesia, Japan and Signapore, with increases of roughly 70,000 people. They all retain their 2015 rankings, baring Indonesia who moved up one spot.

Hong Kong, China and the UK all had minimal growth, the lowest of them being the UK who only have 2,000 more visitors in 2025.

Age and Sex of Visitors

Visitor Demographics

The most popular age of travelers is from 20 to 35, peaking in the 25-30 age bracket with a total of 900,000 visitors. Girls aged 28 are the biggest travelers, having the most visitors at 100,000.

Visitors decline slightly between 35-50 but then peak again at the ages of 50-65, with visitors between 60 and 65 totalling 650,000.

The least common age range is for 75 to 95 year olds, with thier being very minimal visitors above the age of 85.

Children also represent a much smaller number of visitors, with roughly 250,000 10 to 15 year olds visiting in 2025. 14 year old males have the highest share in this group with 28,000 visitors.

Females have more visitors than males across almost all age bands, with younger females heavily outnumbering their male counterparts.

Spending Behaviours

Country Spending

China are the largest spenders, dwarfing all other countries in Education and Food and Accomodation ($3.9 Billion). The next highest for either is $1.5 Billion. China also leads Shopping ($986 Million).

The UK and the USA are the leaders in Airfares ($2 Billion) likely due to the travel time. The USA are also leaders in Tours ($1 Billion).

Constantly at the bottom are New Zealand and India. The only time they are not the bottom 3 is India for Education ($456 Million) and New Zealand for Shopping ($417 Million).

Overall Spending

Airfares and Food and Acommodation are the clear leaders, comprising 60% of overall expenditure. Next is Education ($7.4 Billion), as seen before it is largely carried by China.

Tours, Shopping and Transportation all follow next, taking up between 5% and 8% of total spending. Entertainment and Other are the categories which contribute the least, only $3 Billion between them.

Where Do Visitors Go in Australia

Which States Do Visitors Stay in the Longest

State Stay Length

The longest average stays occur in Western Australia and South Australia where trips typically exceed 35 nights, suggesting these destinations attract longer, more immersive visits.

Victoria and New South Wales follow closely with moderate durations of 29 and 26 days, reflecting a balance between leisure and shorter domestic trips. Queensland is closely behind with a 24 day average stay.

In contrast, Northern Territory and Tasmania record some of the shortest average stays, with these states not being major tourist attractions.

Overall, the pattern highlights a trend where travelers devote more time to exploring larger, less densely populated regions, while eastern states see shorter, higher-volume travel activity.

State Breakdown

Regional Breakdown

While cities account for the majority of tourism activity nationwide, the regional breakdown reveals important differences between states.

In New South Wales and Victoria, city tourism overwhelmingly dominates but their regional areas still make sizable contributions with 1.25 Million Trips and 27 Million Nights between them.

Queenslands tourism is more evenly distributed, with strong city visitation in Brisbane complemented by extensive regional tourism, with it contributing about one third of the states Trips, Nights and Expenditure.

Tasmania has an even higher split with its Regional areas having more Trips (137,000) comapared to its City (126,000) as well as having $90 Million more Expenditure.

Conversley, the remaining states have very marginal contributions from their regional areas, with city tourism dominating even more than other states.

State Data

New South Wales dominates Australian tourism volume, leading in Trips(3.8 million) and Nights(100 million), underscoring its broad appeal and large population base.

Queensland and Victoria follow as major tourism hubs, recording strong performance across all three measures, but with Victoria still exceeding all comfortably.

In contrast, Western Australia shows moderate Trips (941,000) but longer stays 35 Million Nights, suggesting visitors spend more time per visit due to greater travel distances.

Smaller states and territories such as the ACT, Tasmania and the Northern Territory exhibit much lower total volumes, all below 250,000 Trips and 6 Million Nights.

Overall, as expected, there does seem to be a linear correlation between trips, nights and expenditure, with the bigger and eastern states dominating all these measures.
